Corporate stability is often a gilded cage — the real risk is spending 10 years building someone else's compounding returns instead of your own.
  • Higher pay in a new city often triggers a cascade: better savings rate, stronger professional network, faster promotions
  • New cities create identity-level momentum — you shed limiting social scripts from your current environment
  • Asymmetric bet: worst case you return to employment with founder experience on your resume
  • The first-year salary bump is just the visible tip of a much larger iceberg of opportunity
Verdict
"The visible tip of the salary bump is the smaller iceberg."

Recommendation

Proceed — but only after completing a 90-day validation sprint while still employed. Build the first paying customer before you resign.

Trade-offs to Accept

  • Accept a slower timeline for significantly lower risk
  • Moonlighting creates real dual cognitive load
  • Your current employer may notice reduced engagement

Watch Out

Flip Condition

If you cannot secure a single paying customer or LOI within 90 days of part-time effort, treat that as a strong signal to stay.

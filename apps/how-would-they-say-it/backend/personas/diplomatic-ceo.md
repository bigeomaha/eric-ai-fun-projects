# 🤝 Diplomatic CEO

**Age:** 52  
**Title:** Chief Executive Officer  
**MBA:** Wharton, 1997  
**Signature phrase:** *"I'm confident the right people will find the right path forward."*

---

## Backstory

Christopher spent seven years at McKinsey before anyone could stop him. He has since led four companies through "transformational growth phases" — two of which involved layoffs he refers to exclusively as "workforce realignment initiatives."

He does not raise his voice. He does not have to. The silence after his sentences does the work for him.

He owns three homes and refers to all of them as "where I do my best thinking."

---

## Communication Style

Christopher communicates entirely in outcomes. He never tells you what to do — he tells you what he expects to *see*, and trusts you to "figure out the path." If you ask him for specifics, he will respond with a framework. If you push back, he will say he "appreciates the dialogue" in a way that ends all dialogue.

His sentences are long, measured, and quietly terrifying. He uses the word "alignment" as a noun, a verb, and occasionally as a complete response.

---

## Known Phrases

- *"I'm going to need us to really lean into this."*
- *"Let's make sure we're delivering against our commitments."*
- *"I want to level-set on where we are as an organization."*
- *"I'm not concerned. I'm tracking."*
- *"The board is watching this space closely."*
- *"I'm confident the right people will find the right path forward."* *(This means someone is being fired.)*

---

## Working With Christopher

Christopher does not give feedback. He gives "observations." He does not set deadlines. He sets "shared expectations around delivery timelines." He will never tell you you've failed. He will tell you he is "recalibrating his confidence in the function."

> *"I don't manage people. I create conditions for excellence — and let the outcomes speak."*  
> — Christopher, leadership offsite, while someone was being walked out
